Problem statement

Design an original graph feature map \( \phi(G) \) that captures molecular structure and apply it with an SVM kernel of choice, reporting accuracy and F1 on AIDS, PROTEINS, NCI1, PTC‑MR, and MUTAG using a standard CV protocol.

  • Propose \( \phi(G) \), train SVM (e.g., linear/RBF), and justify why the features are informative for classification.
  • Include comparisons to WL and Shortest‑Path; quantum‑inspired ideas and QURI Parts usage are encouraged.

Our innovation

Full‑graph quantum walk embeddings scale qubits with nodes, so we pivot to ego‑graphs that bound circuit width while preserving discriminative local walk dynamics around chemically meaningful centers.

Each ego‑subgraph is encoded by a compact Trotterized circuit using QURI Parts, features are extracted from observables, and aggregated over time points and centers to yield a fixed‑size vector per molecule.

This decouples hardware width from global graph size by capping qubits at the max ego size, enabling NISQ‑feasible circuits without losing salient local patterns.

Overview of feature maps

Feature Map / KernelDescription
QURI Ego‑QW Local ego‑subgraphs are encoded via a fidelity‑style embedding using compact PQCs; features are aggregated across centers and times.
CTQW Features from continuous‑time quantum walk probabilities on the full graph using \( H=\gamma A+V \) or \( H=\gamma L+V \) and \( \psi(t)=e^{-iHt}\psi(0) \).
Shortest‑Path (SP) Histogram over labeled all‑pairs shortest paths \( (\ell_u,\ell_v,d(u,v)) \) as a fixed‑length embedding.
Weisfeiler–Lehman (WL) Color refinement for \( h \) rounds with concatenated histograms \( [\mathrm{hist}(\mathcal{C}^{(0)}),\ldots,\mathrm{hist}(\mathcal{C}^{(h)})] \).

Quantum circuit visualization

The visualization represents the QURI Ego‑QW circuit on 5 qubits corresponding to a radius‑1 ego‑subgraph of a 5‑node star centered at node 0, with gates laid out and wrapped across segments for readability.

Gate layout and wrapping

  • Initialization applies Hadamards to all qubits to build a uniform superposition \( |\!+\rangle^{\otimes n} \).
  • One Trotter step at time \( t=0.5 \) applies per‑edge interactions over edges \( \{(0,1),(0,2),(0,3),(0,4)\} \).

Trotterization

Time evolution under a sum \( H=\sum_k H_k \) is approximated with first‑order Suzuki–Trotter: \( e^{-iHt} \approx \left(\prod_k e^{-iH_k \Delta t}\right)^{t/\Delta t} \), trading error \( O(\Delta t^2) \) for circuit depth.

  • For an ego‑graph, the edge generator implements \( e^{-i\gamma \Delta t (X_iX_j+Y_iY_j)} \) via RXX and RYY with angle \( \phi=2\gamma\Delta t \).
  • Smaller \( \Delta t \) reduces error but increases depth; the design balances fidelity against NISQ feasibility.

Datasets

Benchmarks include AIDS, PROTEINS, NCI1, PTC‑MR, and MUTAG with standardized node labels and consistent splits for evaluation.

Evaluation protocol

  1. Classifier: SVM with RBF kernel.
  2. Validation: Nested 5‑fold CV with inner grid over \( C,\gamma \).
  3. Preprocessing: Per‑graph feature extraction, scaling, and class‑balanced training.
  4. Metrics: Mean ± std of Accuracy and \( F1 \) across outer folds.

Classical baselines

Shortest‑Path (SP)

Embeds graphs via histograms over labeled all‑pairs shortest paths \( (\min(\ell_u,\ell_v),\max(\ell_u,\ell_v),d(u,v)) \), providing a permutation‑invariant summary of global connectivity.

Weisfeiler–Lehman (WL)

Performs \( h \) rounds of color refinement and concatenates color histograms, yielding multi‑scale neighborhood structure features with near‑linear cost per round on sparse graphs.

Tips

  • Normalize histograms and standardize features before SVM; grid‑search \( C,\gamma \) within nested CV.
  • Enrich labels with atomic number, valence, aromaticity, and bond order for better chemical sensitivity.

Key concepts

Graph Laplacian

The combinatorial Laplacian is \( L=D-A \) with degree matrix \( D \) and adjacency \( A \), commonly used to generate diffusion‑like dynamics on graphs.

Hamiltonian choices

Use \( H=\gamma A+V \) or \( H=\gamma L+V \) where \( \gamma \) sets hopping and \( V \) is a diagonal label potential capturing chemistry‑specific node information.
